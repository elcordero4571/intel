Sustainability Through the Ages

Explore Intel's journey through time, discovering how its commitment to innovation has shaped a more sustainable future for technology and our planet.

Intel Sustainability Timeline

Key milestones in Intel's environmental leadership, from early innovation to ambitious climate action goals.

1968

Intel Founded

Robert Noyce and Gordon Moore renamed NM Electronics to Intel Corporation, laying the foundation for future technology and sustainability progress.

1971

First Microprocessor

Intel debuted the 4004, the world's first commercial microprocessor, starting the digital revolution that would later power greener computing solutions.

1978

8086 Processor

The 8086 launched the x86 architecture, which became a backbone of modern PCs and servers that Intel later optimized for energy efficiency.

1985

386 Processor

Intel introduced the 386 processor with 32-bit computing, enabling faster, more capable systems and new sustainability opportunities through improved performance-per-watt.

2006

Peak GHG Emissions

Intel recorded its highest operational greenhouse gas emissions, then invested in renewable energy, chemical abatement, and energy efficiency to reduce its footprint.

2020

RISE Strategy

Intel launched the RISE strategy, committing to responsible, inclusive, sustainable, and enabling practices with clear 2030 goals.

2022

Net-Zero By 2040

Intel announced a plan to achieve net-zero greenhouse gas emissions in its global operations by 2040, building on years of climate work.

2023

Renewable Electricity

Intel reached 99% renewable electricity use worldwide, significantly reducing emissions across its global operations.

2024

Sustainability Summit

Intel hosted its first Sustainability Summit, bringing together industry and government leaders to collaborate on greener manufacturing.
